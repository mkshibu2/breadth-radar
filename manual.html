01 Daily 5-Minute EOD Workflow

After market close (3:30 PM IST), open the dashboard and the sources below in separate tabs. Enter all data into the single EOD Data Entry panel and click Save & Sync All once. That single save updates every indicator on the dashboard automatically.

Step-by-step daily routine
  • 1
    Open NSE Advance/Decline page ~30 sec Go to nseindia.com → Advance/Decline. Enter Advances, Declines, Unchanged for Nifty 500. Then go to 52W High/Low page — enter 52W Highs and 52W Lows.
  • 2
    Open Trendlyne Breadth page ~30 sec Go to Trendlyne Breadth. Enter %>EMA20, %>SMA50, %>SMA200 (three numbers, all as percentages like 28.6).
  • 3
    Enter Nifty 50 Close + Volume ~15 sec Enter today's Nifty 50 closing price and total volume in Crores. The previous close and volume will auto-fill from yesterday's saved entry — verify they look correct. This data automatically powers the MPM (accumulation/distribution calculation) — no separate MPM entry needed for normal days.
  • 4
    Tag today's FTD status (if applicable) ~5 sec If today is the first day the market bounced from a low, select "Day 1 of Rally Attempt". If Nifty rose 1.5%+ on higher volume on Day 4 or later of a rally, select "Follow-Through Day". On normal days, leave blank. See Section 04 for full FTD guide.
  • 5
    Add a note (optional) ~10 sec Type any relevant context — FII buying, RBI policy, Budget day, FTD confirmed. This appears in your 30-day history for future reference.
  • 6
    Click "Save & Sync All" ~5 sec One click saves everything: breadth score, MPM entry, FTD log, and pushes all data to JSONBin cloud. The entire dashboard updates instantly — Band, Analysis Strip, charts, history.
  • 7
    Update BSR if you have open trades ~1 min Go to the BSR Tracker (right column). Click the current price field next to each open trade and update it. The success/failure status and BSR% will recalculate automatically.
Total time: ~5 minutes. The entire routine is designed to be done in one sitting after market close. All indicators update from a single Save.

04 Follow-Through Day (FTD) — Complete Guide

The FTD is O'Neil's most important signal for knowing when a new rally is real. It filters out dead-cat bounces and keeps you out of the market during corrections. Check MarketSmith India's Market Outlook page daily — they classify this for you.

1
Market Low
Market hits a low. Could be the bottom. You don't know yet.
2
Rally Attempt
Index bounces and closes up. This is Day 1. Select "Day 1 of Rally Attempt" in EOD form. No buying yet.
3
Days 2–3
Market must hold above Day 1 low. If it undercuts → attempt failed, restart. Leave dropdown blank.
4+
FTD ✅
Day 4 or later: Nifty closes +1.5% or more on higher volume. Select "Follow-Through Day". Green banner appears. You may now enter.
What to select in the FTD dropdown — daily guide
Market situation todaySelect in dropdownAction
Market bouncing from low for the first time Day 1 of Rally Attempt Start watching. Prepare watchlist. No buying.
Days 2–3 of rally attempt (holding above Day 1 low) Leave blank Continue watching. Do not enter.
Day 4+ with +1.5% on higher volume (check MarketSmith India Market Outlook) Follow-Through Day Green banner activates. Enter pilots (0.25R) on best setups.
Market closes down on heavy volume (any day) Distribution Day Adds to distribution day count. Be alert if this becomes a cluster.
Normal up or neutral day, no special condition Leave blank Routine save. Nothing special.
Critical rule: Do not enter any new breakout trades until the FTD is confirmed — regardless of how good a chart setup looks. O'Neil's research shows that 3 out of 4 breakouts during a correction fail. The FTD is your permission slip.
MarketSmith India shortcut: Their Market Outlook label maps directly to the dropdown. "Confirmed Uptrend" = FTD already happened (select Follow-Through Day if it just changed). "Rally Attempt" = select Day 1. "Uptrend Under Pressure" = leave blank but watch distribution days closely.
05 BSR Tracker — Breakout Success Rate

The BSR Tracker is your real-time feedback loop on whether breakouts are working in the current environment. It tells you what the market is actually doing to your setups — not theory, your own trades.

How to use BSR daily
  • 1
    When you enter a breakout trade Add it to BSR Tracker: Symbol, Pivot price, Breakout date. The age (Early/Mid/Late) and status (Pending) will auto-calculate.
  • 2
    Update current prices daily Click the price field next to each trade in the BSR log and type today's closing price. Success/Fail updates immediately: Success = price ≥ Pivot × 1.05 (+5%). Fail = price < Pivot AND days ≥ 3. Pending = days < 3 (excluded from BSR%).
  • 3
    Read the BSR% in the band This is your permission dial for position sizing. See the table below.
BSR% → Position sizing action
BSR %EnvironmentAction
≥80%Strong — breakouts running wellFull size entries. Add at E2/E3 aggressively.
65–80%Healthy — most setups workingFull size entries. Add on strength.
50–65%Mixed — selective participationReduce size to 0.5R. Only A+ setups.
30–50%Weak — market not rewarding breakoutsPilot entries only (0.25R). No adds.
<30%Avoid — breakouts failing consistentlyStop entering breakouts. Manage existing positions.
Your rule: Add to a position only within 2.5% of pivot. Never let a 5%+ winner become a loss. Your BSR history will tell you when the market consistently respects these rules.
06 Market Pressure Meter (MPM)

MPM tracks institutional activity on Nifty 50. Accumulation days = Close up AND Volume up (institutions buying into strength). Distribution days = Close down AND Volume up (institutions selling into weakness). Neutral = volume lower than prior day.

MPM Score interpretation — Last 25 trading days
Score (Acc − Dist)ConditionImplication
≥ +5Strong BuyingInstitutions accumulating. Rally likely sustainable.
+2 to +4HealthyMore buying than selling. Positive backdrop.
0 to +1NeutralNo strong signal either way. Use breadth score as primary.
−1 to −3WeakDistribution starting. Caution on new entries.
≤ −4Heavy SellingInstitutional selling dominant. No new trades. Tighten stops.

Auto-fill from EOD entry: When you enter Nifty Close and Volume in the EOD form and click Save, the system automatically classifies the day as Acc/Dist/Neutral and adds it to MPM — no manual MPM entry needed for normal days. The MPM form in the right column is only needed for historical backdating or corrections.

Distribution Day Alert: If the Analysis Strip shows 4+ distribution days, avoid opening new positions. At 6+ distribution days, O'Neil's research shows a correction is typically already underway or imminent. Begin tightening stops on all open trades.
07 Position Sizing — ₹5,00,000 Capital

Your fixed rules: Max risk = 1% of capital = ₹5,000 per trade. Position size is determined by stop-loss distance from pivot. The breadth zone then scales your actual risk deployed.

Position sizing formula

Risk per share = Entry Price × SL%    |    Quantity = ₹5,000 ÷ Risk per share

Example: Stock at ₹500, SL at 7% below entry → Risk/share = ₹35 → Qty = 5000 ÷ 35 = 142 shares → Entry value ≈ ₹71,000 (14.2% of capital in one trade)

08 Cloud Sync — Access from Any Device

All your data is stored in two places: browser localStorage (instant, offline) and JSONBin.io (cloud, syncs across devices). Setup is one-time per device.

First-time setup on a new device
  • 1
    Create a JSONBin account (first time only)Go to jsonbin.io → Sign up (free) → API Keys → copy your Master Key.
  • 2
    Connect on first deviceClick the sync status button in the top-right of the dashboard header → paste your Master Key → click "Connect & Create Bin". A Bin ID is created automatically and saved.
  • 3
    On second deviceOpen the dashboard → click sync button → paste the same Master Key → the Bin ID will auto-fill. Click "↓ Pull" to fetch all your data from the cloud.
  • 4
    Daily syncEvery time you click "Save & Sync All" in the EOD form, data is automatically pushed to the cloud. On other devices, click "↓ Pull" to get the latest. Or click "↑ Push" to force-upload from the current device.
What syncs: Breadth history (30 days) · BSR trades · MPM daily log · FTD rally log. All in a single JSONBin. Your API key is stored only in each browser's localStorage — it is never sent anywhere except JSONBin's own servers.
