The Trading Model - Shibu

1. Environment Analysis

Breadth Score (0–100): Composite measure of market participation.

ConditionPhaseModeAction
BS > 55 & NH > NL Healthy Bull Aggressive (BS > 55) Trade freely, full position size, focus on breakouts
BS 45–55 Expansion Selective (BS 45–55) Only best setups, moderate size, avoid chasing
BS 35–45 Recovery Controlled (BS 35–45) Pilot entries only (0.25R–0.5R), test strength
BS < 35 Danger Defensive (BS < 35) No new trades, stay in cash, protect capital

2. Stock Screening

  • Price above 10EMA, 20EMA, 150SMA, 200SMA
  • Within 25% of 52W high
  • At least 25% above 52W low
  • RS ≥ 80
  • Earnings & Sales growth (last 3 quarters)

3. Stock Selection (Detailed Criteria)

  • Structure: Tight consolidation / base (at least 3–6 weeks), clear resistance level
  • Trend Alignment: 10EMA > 20EMA > 150SMA > 200SMA
  • Volume Behavior: Contraction during base, expansion on breakout
  • Relative Strength: RS ≥ 80 and outperforming index
  • Sector Strength: Prefer leading sectors with multiple strong stocks
  • Price Location: Near 52-week high (within 25%)
  • Avoid: Loose structure, wide candles, erratic volume, laggards

4. Position Sizing & R System

ModeAllocationPer Trade
Aggressive80–100%Full
Selective40–60%Half
Defensive0–20%Minimal
  • 1R = Risk per trade (Max 1% of capital)
  • Stop Loss defines 1R (Example: 5% SL = 1R)
  • 0.25R → Very small position (testing)
  • 0.5R → Pilot position
  • 1R → Full risk position
  • Never exceed 1R total risk per trade

5. Entry (Pilot & Add-On Method)

  • Preferred Setups:
    • Breakout from consolidation / base
    • Volatility contraction pattern (VCP)
    • Tight flag / pullback to 10 EMA
    • First pullback after breakout
  • Pilot Entry: 25–50% at breakout
  • Add-On: After confirmation or pullback support
  • Avoid extended entries (>5% from pivot)

6. Stop Loss & Trailing System

  • Initial SL:
    • Max 7% or below breakout / swing low
    • Defines 1R
  • Move to Break Even:
    • After price moves ~1R in favor
    • Or strong breakout follow-through
  • Trailing Stop:
    • Primary: 10 EMA (hold as long as price respects it)
    • Secondary: 20 EMA (final support)
    • For positional trades: 10 Week EMA
  • Tighten SL:
    • After strong vertical move
    • When market weakens

7. Trade Management

  • Holding Winners:
    • Respect pullbacks to 10 EMA (healthy trend)
    • Volume contraction during pullback indicates strength
    • Support near 10 EMA / breakout level
    • Stronger trends may hold 10 Week EMA
  • Extended Stock (Definition):
    • Price 10–15% above breakout level
    • Far from 10 EMA
    • Fast vertical move without consolidation
  • Parabolic Move:
    • Sharp, near-vertical price rise in short time
    • Successive large bullish candles
    • High risk of reversal
  • Exhaustion Gap:
    • Gap up after extended run
    • Occurs near top of move
    • Often followed by reversal or consolidation
  • Profit Booking:
    • Take partial profits when stock becomes extended
    • If parabolic move appears → book aggressively
    • If exhaustion gap forms → consider exit or partial exit
    • If price shows weakness after strong move → lock profits
  • Scaling (Adding Positions):
    • Add only after price confirms strength
    • Confirmation = higher high + strong close + volume
    • Add near pullback support (10 EMA) or new breakout
    • Each add should be smaller than previous (risk control)
    • Never add to losing trades

8. Exit Rules

  • Hard Exit (Immediate):
    • Stop loss hit (max 1R)
    • Breakdown below key support with volume
  • Technical Exit:
    • Strong close below 10 EMA → partial or full exit
    • Break below 20 EMA with weakness → full exit
  • Profit Based Exit:
    • 1. Gain < 10% - Use original stop.
    • 2. Gain 10–15% - Stop cannot be below cost. (trade should not become a loser. )
    • 3. Gain 15–20% - Trail below swing low or 20EMA.
    • 4. Gain > 20% - Trail more aggressively using: 10EMA, swing lows, or volatility adjusted stops.
  • Market Exit (Override Rule):
    • If Breadth weakens (BS < 35)
    • Exit weak positions immediately
    • Tighten SL on all positions
  • Climactic / Extended Exit:
    • Stock becomes 10–15% extended from base
    • Sharp vertical move (parabolic)
    • Book partial or full profits
  • Time-Based Exit:
    • No progress after reasonable time (5–10 sessions)
    • Exit and redeploy capital
  • "Very Extended From 10EMA": li>
    • Price is: 15%+ above 10EMA for Large caps
    • 20%+ above 10EMA for Mid/Small caps
    • AND preferably:
    • • Breadth deteriorating
    • • Stock showing climactic behaviour
    • • Vertical advance in short time

9. Re-entry

  • New setup forms
  • Breakout again
  • Market supports

10. No Trade Condition

  • Breadth Score < 35 → Strict NO TRADE
  • Market participation is weak
  • Breakouts are likely to fail
  • Capital protection is priority
  • Only exception: existing trades with strong trend (manage tightly)

11. Daily Execution Routine

  • Step 1: Check Breadth Score
    • Identify Market Phase (Bull / Expansion / Recovery / Danger)
    • Decide Mode (Aggressive / Selective / Controlled / Defensive)
  • Step 2: Define Risk & Position Size
    • Aggressive → Up to 1R
    • Selective → 0.5R
    • Recovery → 0.25R–0.5R
    • Danger → No trades
  • Step 3: Scan Market
    • Filter stocks using screening criteria
    • Focus only on strong sectors
  • Step 4: Select Setups
    • Identify valid bases and breakouts
    • Avoid extended or weak structures
  • Step 5: Execute or Stay Out
    • If all conditions align → Enter trade
    • If not → Stay in cash
  • Step 6: Manage Open Trades
    • Trail using 10 EMA
    • Watch 20 EMA for deeper weakness
    • Adjust based on market condition

Core Rule

Trade when market allows. Stay out when it doesn’t.
