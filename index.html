    About This Tool
    NSE Breadth Radar is a personal market breadth dashboard built for Indian swing traders who follow price-and-volume methodology. It tracks market internals — advance/decline ratios, moving average breadth, 52-week high/low ratios, breakout success rates and distribution pressure — to give you a single Breadth Score and clear zone guidance before you place any trade.

    Built from scratch out of a trader's real need. Free to use. Not investment advice.

    Sector Rotation Module
    The Sector Rotation Module tracks 16 NSE sectoral indices — from IT and Pharma to Defence, Capital Markets, and PSE — and classifies each into one of four Weinstein-style stages based on its position relative to its 20-week and 50-week moving averages.

    How to read it: Every sector row shows its % above/below the 20-week MA (vs20), MA dot indicators (● above · ◐ within 1% · ○ below), stage badge (S2 Advancing · S1 Basing · S3 Topping · S4 Declining), relative strength vs Nifty 50 (RS%), and weeks in the current stage.

    Trading edge: Focus new breakout entries on S2 sectors — they are above both MAs and in an advancing trend. S1 sectors are basing and warrant watchlist attention. Avoid S3/S4 for new longs.

    Click any sector name to open its live NSE index tracker page with constituent stocks and performance data. Data is updated weekly (Friday/Sunday). Use the bulk import to enter historical weekly closes for up to 16 sectors at once.

    Relative Rotation Graph (RRG)
    The RRG is a four-quadrant scatter chart that plots every sector simultaneously using two metrics: RS-Ratio (X-axis) measures whether the sector is stronger or weaker than Nifty 50 on a smoothed basis, and RS-Momentum (Y-axis) measures whether that relative strength is improving or deteriorating.

    The four quadrants: Leading (strong & improving, top-right) → Weakening (strong but slowing, bottom-right) → Lagging (weak & deteriorating, bottom-left) → Improving (gaining momentum, top-left). Clockwise rotation is the natural bullish path.

    Tail: Each sector plots a coloured trail showing its last N weeks of movement — you can visually see direction, not just position. Sectors with long tails moving toward the Leading quadrant are the strongest candidates.

    Controls: Use + / − or preset buttons (4 · 8 · 12 · All) to control how many sectors appear. The Weeks stepper controls tail length (1–52 weeks). Filter: S2 shows only advancing sectors. Highlight chips (left-click = spotlight, right-click = hide) let you isolate one or more sectors. The ▶ Play animation replays each sector's journey week-by-week so you can see momentum build up over time.
